# Joshua Follow-Up — Presentation Spec

**Presenter:** Owen  
**Audience:** Joshua, Igor, MJ  
**Primary Goal:** Structure the engagement discussion so the meeting ends with a clear path to a written phase-1 proposal  
**Secondary Goals:** Signal seriousness and momentum, frame the work as staged feasibility rather than a rushed build, and make USEFULL's staffing and boundary conditions explicit  
**Format:** Short discussion deck  
**Recommended Length:** 7-9 slides

---

## Tone

- Direct
- Professional
- Collaborative
- Serious without overcommitting
- Clear that USEFULL wants to move, but in a disciplined way

---

## Core Framing To Preserve

- USEFULL has decided to pursue Path A: engage the consultant
- This is not a September launch push
- The first phase should be feasibility/discovery, not productionization
- Owen can support at roughly 20-30% allocation
- Nadia should not be involved in this phase
- A dev resource may come through Igor, but that still needs clarification
- The first contract should help USEFULL determine whether and how this concept is worth advancing

---

## What This Meeting Is For

- Align on the purpose and shape of a first consulting phase
- Clarify roles, constraints, and expectations
- Identify what Joshua needs from USEFULL to scope the work
- Agree on the process and timeline for getting to a proposal and contract

## What This Meeting Is Not For

- Finalizing the technical architecture
- Committing to a production rollout plan
- Locking detailed implementation milestones
- Re-debating the full research case from the internal deck

---

## Recommended Slide Outline

### Slide 1: Title / Meeting Purpose
- Title: `Joshua Follow-Up`
- Subtitle: `Structuring a Phase-1 Consulting Engagement`
- One-line objective: align on the shape of an initial engagement and the path to a scoped proposal

### Slide 2: Why We’re Moving Forward
- Leadership is interested in pursuing this path
- USEFULL wants to begin now without forcing a September delivery
- A staged approach reduces risk before heavier internal investment

### Slide 3: Decision So Far
- USEFULL is choosing Path A: engage Joshua
- This is a feasibility-first effort, not a production commitment
- The initial phase should focus on learning, prototyping, and narrowing uncertainty

### Slide 4: Working Constraints
- Owen available at roughly 20-30%
- No Nadia allocation in this phase
- Likely implementation support via Igor / a dev resource
- Avoid disruption to existing roadmap priorities
- Prefer a scoped first phase with clear boundaries

### Slide 5: What Phase 1 Should Accomplish
- Clarify the right scope for early feasibility work
- Identify the main technical and operational risks
- Define what kind of early prototype or proof work makes sense first
- Establish what people, hardware, and access are needed
- Produce enough clarity for USEFULL to decide whether to continue and how

### Slide 6: Questions We Need Joshua To Help Answer
- What should the first engagement actually cover?
- What role should Joshua play: advisor, hands-on consultant, technical lead over another dev, or hybrid?
- What inputs would Joshua need from USEFULL to scope phase 1?
- What should a useful proposal from him include?
- What timeline and sequencing would be realistic for a first phase?

### Slide 7: Resourcing / Operating Model
- Joshua: consulting lead and technical feasibility partner
- Owen: PM, coordination, research continuity, and internal owner
- Igor: technical advisor and possible source of dev support
- MJ / leadership: business constraints, budget, and operational alignment
- Explicit note: product engineering integration is not phase-1 scope

### Slide 8: Desired Outcome From Today
- Shared understanding of the first contract’s purpose
- Agreement on what Joshua will include in a proposal
- Agreement on what USEFULL will send after the meeting
- Proposal due date
- Follow-up review meeting scheduled

### Slide 9: Next Steps / Owners
- Joshua: draft proposal / scope outline
- Owen: send follow-up notes and materials
- Igor: confirm dev-resource posture
- USEFULL leadership: confirm budget guardrails and review path
- Schedule proposal review meeting

## Content Guidance

- Keep technical content high-level and minimal
- Do not re-present the full throat concept deck
- Use prior research as context, not as the subject of this meeting
- Keep the deck focused on engagement structure, roles, scope, constraints, and process
- If the technical concept is mentioned, keep it to one sentence

---

## Suggested Closing Line

`We want to leave this meeting aligned on what a sensible phase-1 engagement would be, what you need from us to scope it, and how we get from here to a written proposal.`
